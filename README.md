## Overview
This research presents a longitudinal, plant-level dataset linking detailed financial data from the **Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) Form 1** with physical generator characteristics from the **U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA)**. 

The analysis demonstrates the value and limitations of public power plant cost data and provides an empirical analysis of capital and operating costs for the U.S. regulated power fleet between 1994 and 2023.

### Key Features
* **Harmonized Dataset**: A longitudinal dataset linking FERC financial records to EIA physical attributes.
* **Cost Analysis**: Detailed breakdowns of Capital Expenditures (CapEx) and Operating Expenses (OpEx) across technologies (Coal, NGCC, Nuclear, etc.).
* **Environmental Case Study**: Estimation of the impacts of EPA environmental regulations on coal plant capital investment.
* **PUDL Foundation**: Built on the [Public Utility Data Liberation (PUDL)](https://catalyst.coop/pudl/) project by Catalyst Cooperative.

## Data Sources
* **FERC Form 1**: Plant-level financial data (Schedule 402).
* **EIA Form 860**: Generator-level physical and operational attributes.
* **Catalyst Cooperative (PUDL)**: Initial data processing and access.
